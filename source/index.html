Promoting Responsible Data Leadership

Recent years have seen an uptick in the use of private-sector data and analytical expertise for public good through Data Collaboratives – the cross-sector exchange of data and data science expertise to create new public value.

Around the world and across sectors, the private sector – from SMEs to international civil society organizations to multinational corporations – are collaborating with the public sector, academia and civil society in leveraging their data to address some of society’s biggest challenges.

 

More about Data Collaboratives

Through our Data Collaboratives Initiative we learned that while data collaboratives are capable of creating public value, they require significant and time-consuming efforts from both data holders on the supply side, and institutions that represent the demand.

To find ways to lower the high transaction costs involved, and to amplify the positive impacts of cross-sector data sharing for the public good, the GovLab, with generous support from the William and Flora Hewlett Foundation is documenting and seeking to advance the concept of Data Stewardship to promote responsible data leadership and to make Data Collaboratives more sustainable, systematic and responsible. Bookmark datastewards.net as we release new insights, tools and calls over the next few months

Making Data Collaboratives Sustainable, Systematic and Responsible

Together with BrightFront Group and Adapt, we are:

  • Exploring the need and current practice of Data Stewards within the private sector who can act as change agents responsible for determining what, when, how and with whom to share private data for public good, and to subsequently ensure insights are acted upon.
  • Identifying and Connecting existing Data Stewards across sectors and regions to create a Network that can exchange lessons learned, establish common norms and procedures, through a series of online and in-person convenings;
  • Developing methodologies, tools and frameworks to use data more responsibly and systematically and to decrease the transaction cost, time and energy currently needed to establish Data Collaboratives in a responsible manner.

Are you a Data Steward? Do you help drive decision-making regarding if, when, and how to share the data your corporation or organization holds for the public benefit? Are you interested in joining a community of practice and learn from your peers regarding their approaches for data sharing while sharing your expertise and experience?
